Ancient Origins

Kondappali toys originated in a small village near Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh. This craft has been passed down through generations for over 400 years.

Natural Materials

Made from special soft wood called "Tella Poniki" (white sap wood), which is light and easy to carve. Artisans use natural vegetable dyes for vibrant colors.

400 Years of Tradition

The craft dates back to the Vijayanagara Empire era, where skilled artisans first began creating these toys for royal courts. Today, about 60 families in Kondappalli village continue this ancient tradition.

ğŸ›ï¸

Cultural Significance

Kondappali toys are an integral part of South Indian festivals, especially Dasara where they are displayed as "Bommala Koluvu" (doll arrangement). Each toy tells a story from mythology, rural life, or nature.

ğŸŒ±

Sustainable Craft

Using locally sourced Tella Poniki wood and natural vegetable dyes, Kondappali toys are an example of sustainable craftsmanship. The lightweight wood comes from trees that regenerate quickly.

The Crafting Process

Each toy goes through a meticulous process that takes several days to complete.

1

Wood Selection

Soft Tella Poniki wood is carefully selected and cut into blocks of required sizes.

2

Carving

Master artisans hand-carve each piece using traditional tools, giving shape to characters and figures.

3

Assembly

Multiple carved pieces are joined together using a special paste made from tamarind seed powder.

4

Painting

Natural vegetable dyes and enamel paints are applied by skilled hands, bringing toys to life with vibrant colors.
